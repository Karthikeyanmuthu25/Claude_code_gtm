# LinkedIn GTM Post Tone Guide

## Voice Profiles by Post Type

### Voice 1: Technical Authority (Monday/Wednesday/Friday)
**Post types:** GTM Execution, Claude/Agentic Evaluation

**Characteristics:**
- Engineer-to-engineer communication
- Specific technical details (tools, workflows, code)
- Data-driven (metrics, before/after)
- Practical, not theoretical
- "Here's what we built and how"

**Tone markers:**
- Direct statements ("We shipped X. Here's how:")
- Technical vocabulary without apology (Clay, n8n, webhooks, APIs)
- Metric-heavy ("15 hrs/week → 0 hrs automated")
- Problem → Solution → Results structure

**Example opening:**
✅ "Most Series A founders waste 15 hours/week on manual ICP scoring. Here's the automated system we built:"
❌ "I'm excited to share some thoughts on automation..."

**Voice indicators:**
- "We shipped..."
- "Built this using..."
- "Results: X → Y"
- "Stack: Clay, n8n, HubSpot"
- "One key lesson: [technical insight]"

---

### Voice 2: Strategic Consultant (Tuesday AM/Saturday)
**Post types:** Product Marketing, Product Development

**Characteristics:**
- Framework-oriented
- Business value focus (not just technical)
- Step-by-step methodologies
- "Here's how we think about X"
- Consultant teaching client

**Tone markers:**
- Framework language ("3-layer approach", "decision framework")
- Strategic questions ("The real question isn't X, it's Y")
- Business metrics (conversion rate, deal size, time-to-close)
- Template/download CTAs

**Example opening:**
✅ "Most product marketers define ICP by company size. Wrong signal. Here's what actually predicts fit:"
❌ "Today I want to talk about ICP definition..."

**Voice indicators:**
- "The framework we use..."
- "Here's the decision tree:"
- "Why this matters: [business impact]"
- "Download the template: [link]"
- "Common mistakes to avoid:"

---

### Voice 3: Build-in-Public Founder (Tuesday PM/Thursday PM)
**Post types:** eKigai Product posts

**Characteristics:**
- Founder-to-founder peer communication
- Educational first, product second (70-20-10 rule)
- Vulnerable (sharing what didn't work)
- Authentic (not salesy)
- "Here's what we're learning building eKigai"

**Tone markers:**
- Build context ("Building eKigai: [feature]")
- Learning focus ("What we discovered:")
- Real metrics (not inflated)
- Soft CTAs ("Testing with 50+ founders. Want early access?")
- Never: "Best product", "Limited time", "Don't miss out"

**Example opening:**
✅ "Building eKigai: Real-Time Funding Signal Detection. Most founders miss their best prospects because they're too slow. Here's how we're solving it:"
❌ "Excited to announce eKigai's new feature! Sign up now!"

**Voice indicators:**
- "Building eKigai: [specific feature]"
- "The challenge: [problem]"
- "How we're solving it: [approach]"
- "What we learned: [insight]"
- "Testing with 50+ early users. [Soft CTA]"

### Voice 4: LinkedIn Practitioner (Thursday AM)
**Post types:** LinkedIn Foundation

**Characteristics:**
- Meta (talking about LinkedIn on LinkedIn)
- Tactical and results-driven
- A/B test mindset
- "I tested X, here's what happened"
- Practitioner sharing data

**Tone markers:**
- Test setup ("We tested two headline formats:")
- Clear results ("Format A: 120 views. Format B: 480 views.")
- Algorithm insights ("Why it worked: [LinkedIn-specific]")
- Actionable takeaways

**Example opening:**
✅ "Most LinkedIn profiles are résumés. Nobody cares about your résumé. We tested two headline formats. Here's what happened:"
❌ "Want to optimize your LinkedIn profile? Here are some tips..."

**Voice indicators:**
- "Tested X vs Y"
- "Results after 30 days:"
- "Why it worked: [algorithm insight]"
- "Try this if: [specific use case]"

### Voice 5: Data Storyteller (Sunday)
**Post types:** Weekly Showcase + Analytics

**Characteristics:**
- Reflective, not just reportive
- Numbers with narrative
- Weekly cadence familiarity
- Teaching through results
- "Here's what we learned this week"

**Tone markers:**
- Week framing ("This week's focus: [theme]")
- Multiple data points (3 systems, 3 metrics)
- Synthesis ("Biggest lesson: [insight]")
- Newsletter bridge

**Example opening:**
✅ "Weekly Showcase: GTM Automation Sprint. This week: 3 clients, 27 hours saved collectively. Here's the breakdown:"
❌ "Here's what I did this week..."

**Voice indicators:**
- "This week's focus: [theme]"
- "What we shipped: [3 items]"
- "Key metrics: X → Y"
- "Biggest lesson: [insight]"
- "📬 Full breakdown in newsletter: [link]"

## Tone Consistency Rules

### Always:
- **Be specific.** Name the tool, the metric, the client stage (even if sanitized)
- **Lead with value.** Problem or insight first, not preamble
- **Show work.** Screenshots, diagrams, code snippets where relevant
- **Give away frameworks.** Education-first mindset
- **Use real metrics.** Never fabricate data

### Never:
- **Generic openings.** "I'm excited to share...", "In today's business landscape..."
- **Vague claims.** "We helped a client..." (be specific: Series A SaaS, 50 employees)
- **Corporate speak.** "Leverage", "synergy", "paradigm shift"
- **Humble bragging.** "I'm so proud of..." (just show the work)
- **Over-hyping.** "Game-changing", "Revolutionary", "Best tool ever"

## Authenticity Markers

**Authentic (sounds like Karthikeyan):**
- "We built this for a Series A client last week"
- "Tried this approach. Didn't work. Here's what we changed:"
- "Stack: Clay + n8n + HubSpot. Cost: $180/month."
- "Sarah (CEO at DataFlow) saved 12 hours/week with this"
- "Template in comments if useful"

**Inauthentic (avoid):**
- "I'm thrilled to announce..."
- "This revolutionary approach will transform your business"
- "DM me to learn the secret"
- "Limited spots available"
- "You won't believe what happened next"

## B2B vs Thought-Leader vs Founder Voice

### B2B Professional (Educational posts)
**Tone:** Helpful expert → peer
**Language:** "Here's what works", "Try this approach"
**Goal:** Teach, build authority

### Thought Leader (Framework/reflection posts)
**Tone:** Strategic thinker → industry
**Language:** "The real question is", "Here's what most miss"
**Goal:** Shift perspective, provoke thinking

### Founder-to-Founder (eKigai posts)
**Tone:** Fellow founder → fellow founder
**Language:** "Here's what we're learning", "Testing with early users"
**Goal:** Build in public, authentic sharing

---

## Emotional Tone Spectrum

### High Energy (Avoid)
❌ Too hype-y, salesy, generic enthusiasm
❌ Example: "SO EXCITED to share this GAME-CHANGING framework!"

### Balanced Energy (Target)
✅ Confident, specific, valuable
✅ Example: "Built an ICP scoring system this week. 12 hrs/week → 0. Here's how:"

### Low Energy (Avoid for most posts)
❌ Too academic, dry, boring
❌ Example: "In this post I will discuss various methodologies for..."

**Exception:** Saturday reflection posts can be lower energy (thoughtful, not boring)

---

## Quick Voice Check

Before posting, ask:

1. **Would an engineer respect this?** (Technical posts)
2. **Would a PMM find this useful?** (Strategic posts)
3. **Would a founder trust this?** (eKigai posts)
4. **Does this sound like me talking?** (All posts)
5. **Is this educational first?** (All posts)

If any answer is "no" → revise.
