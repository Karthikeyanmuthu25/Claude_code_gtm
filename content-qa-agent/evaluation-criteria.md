# Content QA Evaluation Criteria
## Detailed Scoring Rubric

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** March 11, 2026

---

## CATEGORY 1: HOOK ANALYSIS (0-3 points)

### Definition
The hook is the first line or opening that determines whether someone keeps reading. On LinkedIn, you have ~1 second to capture attention.

---

### SCORING RUBRIC

#### 3/3 Points: Exceptional Hook ⭐⭐⭐
**Criteria:**
- Uses proven high-performing pattern
- Highly specific (numbers, names, concrete details)
- Creates strong curiosity gap
- Promises clear, valuable takeaway
- Audience immediately knows "this is for me"

**Examples:**
```
✅ "I tested 3 AI lead qualification tools. Only 1 hit 90% accuracy. Here's the winner and why."
   - Specific number (3 tools)
   - Concrete result (90% accuracy)
   - Clear promise (winner + reasoning)

✅ "Day 47/90: My $800/month AI tool got replaced by a $40/month custom solution."
   - Progress indicator (builds trust)
   - Shocking comparison ($800 vs $40)
   - Implies lesson learned

✅ "RAG systems fail 40% of the time in production. Here's why yours will too."
   - Controversial stat (40% failure)
   - Direct address (yours)
   - Promises explanation
```

---

#### 2/3 Points: Good Hook ⭐⭐
**Criteria:**
- Uses recognizable pattern but not perfectly
- Somewhat specific but could be more concrete
- Creates mild curiosity
- Value is present but not compelling
- Audience can identify relevance with effort

**Examples:**
```
⚠️ "Building AI systems is hard. Here's what I learned."
   - Generic opening (everyone says this)
   + Clear promise (lessons learned)
   - No specific hook about WHAT makes it hard

⚠️ "Most companies waste money on AI tools. Here's how to avoid it."
   + Identifies problem
   - "Most companies" is vague
   - "Waste money" needs specifics

⚠️ "I spent 3 months building a RAG system. Here's the result."
   + Timeframe (3 months)
   - No result teased (what IS the result?)
```

---

#### 1/3 Points: Weak Hook ⭐
**Criteria:**
- Generic or overused opening
- Vague or no specifics
- Minimal curiosity created
- Value unclear
- Could apply to any content

**Examples:**
```
❌ "AI is changing GTM. Here's what you need to know."
   - Most generic AI hook possible
   - "What you need to know" = vague promise

❌ "Let me share my thoughts on vector databases."
   - No reason to care about your thoughts
   - "Share" implies talking AT reader

❌ "I've been working with LLMs for a while now."
   - "A while" = vague
   - No value statement
   - Who cares?
```

---

#### 0/3 Points: No Hook / Bad Hook 💀
**Criteria:**
- No attempt at hook (jumps straight to content)
- Actively bad hook that pushes readers away
- Confusing or misleading opening
- Question with no curiosity gap

**Examples:**
```
🚫 "In this post, I will discuss..."
   - Academic writing, not social media
   
🚫 "Hey everyone! Hope you're having a great day!"
   - Wastes reader's time
   - No value signal

🚫 "Thought I'd share this..."
   - Implies you're unsure if it's valuable
   
🚫 "What is RAG?" [Then explains RAG generically]
   - Question answered everywhere
   - No unique angle
```

---

### HOOK PATTERN LIBRARY

#### Pattern 1: Specific Stat/Result (Most Reliable)
```
Template: "I [action] [number] [thing]. [Surprising result]. Here's [insight]."

Examples:
✅ "I tested 5 vector databases. Weaviate beat Pinecone by 40ms. Here's the setup."
✅ "I cold-emailed 200 AI companies. 47 responded. Here's the template."
✅ "Our RAG system went from 73% to 94% accuracy. Changed one thing."
```

**When to Use:** When you have concrete data, experiments, or measurable results.

---

#### Pattern 2: Bold Claim with Proof
```
Template: "[Controversial statement]. Here's why."

Examples:
✅ "LangChain is slowing down your RAG system. Here's the proof."
✅ "Most AI qualification tools are worse than random. I tested 8."
✅ "You don't need fine-tuning. RAG solves 90% of use cases."
```

**When to Use:** When you have a strong opinion backed by evidence.

---

#### Pattern 3: Contrarian Take
```
Template: "Stop [common practice]. [Better alternative] instead."

Examples:
✅ "Stop paying $800/month for Pinecone. Here's the $40 alternative."
✅ "Stop building AI agents with LangChain. Use LangGraph instead."
✅ "Stop chasing product-market fit. Build signal-market fit first."
```

**When to Use:** When you disagree with common advice and have better approach.

---

#### Pattern 4: Progress/Journey Update
```
Template: "Day X/Y: [Milestone achieved]. [Key insight]."

Examples:
✅ "Day 47/90: Shipped my first multi-agent system. It broke immediately. Here's why."
✅ "Week 8/12: Generated $10K in pipeline using AI qualification. Here's the system."
✅ "Month 3: My LinkedIn following went from 200 to 2,400. The strategy:"
```

**When to Use:** When building in public and have regular updates.

---

#### Pattern 5: Problem-Solution Tease
```
Template: "Problem: [pain]. Solution: [approach]. Result: [outcome]."

Examples:
✅ "Problem: Manual lead qualification took 2 hours/day. Solution: RAG-powered agent. Result: 30 seconds per lead."
✅ "Problem: Our RAG system hallucinated 30% of answers. Solution: Hybrid search. Result: 4% hallucination rate."
```

**When to Use:** When you solved a specific, relatable problem.

---

#### Pattern 6: Before/After Comparison
```
Template: "Before: [bad state]. After: [good state]. How: [method]."

Examples:
✅ "Before: $800/month for Clay. After: $40/month custom RAG. How: 3-day build."
✅ "Before: 20% email response rate. After: 67% response rate. How: AI personalization."
```

**When to Use:** When you have dramatic improvement to showcase.

---

### RED FLAGS (Auto-Deduct Points)

**Instant 0-1 Score Triggers:**
- "AI is changing [industry]..."
- "In today's world..."
- "As we all know..."
- "I wanted to share..."
- "Thought I'd post about..."
- "Let me tell you about..."
- "Here's my take on..."
- Generic question: "What is [obvious thing]?"

## CATEGORY 2: FLOW ANALYSIS (0-3 points)

### Definition
Flow is how content progresses from hook to CTA. Good flow feels inevitable - each line leads naturally to the next.

---

### SCORING RUBRIC

#### 3/3 Points: Excellent Flow ⭐⭐⭐
**Criteria:**
- Clear structure (recognizable pattern)
- Smooth transitions (no jarring jumps)
- Progressive disclosure (simple → complex)
- Scannable formatting (line breaks, emojis, bullets)
- Perfect pacing (not too fast or slow)
- Each paragraph serves purpose
- Varied sentence length (rhythm)

**Example:**
```
✅ EXCELLENT FLOW:

Day 23/90: My RAG system failed in production. [HOOK]

The error: "IndexError: list index out of range" [PROBLEM]

The cause: Chunking strategy was creating empty chunks. [ROOT CAUSE]

The fix: [SOLUTION - SCANNABLE]
→ Skip chunks <50 tokens
→ Remove whitespace-only chunks  
→ Log all skipped chunks for review

Result: Zero errors in 48 hours, 1,247 queries. [PROOF]

Tomorrow: Multi-LLM consensus strategies. [PREVIEW]

Follow if you're building production AI. [CTA]
```

**Why This Works:**
- PAS structure (Problem → Agitate → Solution)
- Code-style formatting (scannable)
- Each line adds value
- No wasted words
- Clear progression

---

#### 2/3 Points: Good Flow ⭐⭐
**Criteria:**
- Structure is present but not perfect
- Most transitions work
- Some formatting issues (walls of text)
- Pacing slightly off
- Minor redundancy

**Example:**
```
⚠️ GOOD FLOW (Needs Polish):

I built a RAG system for lead qualification.

It works by embedding your ICP criteria in a vector database. Then when you get a new lead, it retrieves the relevant criteria and scores the lead against your requirements. This is better than generic scoring rules because it uses your exact criteria.

The system runs on Pinecone and Claude API. Cost is about $0.03 per lead.

Results have been good so far. About 91% accuracy compared to manual scoring.

If you want to build something similar, let me know.
```

**Issues:**
- Paragraph 2 is wall of text (needs line breaks)
- "About 91%" is vague (should be exact)
- "Results have been good" is weak (show data)
- CTA is passive ("let me know")

**Fix:**
Break into scannable format, add specifics, strengthen CTA.

---

#### 1/3 Points: Weak Flow ⭐
**Criteria:**
- Unclear structure
- Jarring transitions
- Random order of ideas
- Wall of text formatting
- Repetitive or circular

**Example:**
```
❌ WEAK FLOW:

I've been working on AI systems lately. RAG is interesting because you can use it for a lot of things. Vector databases store embeddings which are representations of text as numbers.

AI is changing how we do GTM. Also, I think LangChain is useful but sometimes slow. Yesterday I was debugging an error in my code.

Pinecone is expensive but easy to use. Follow for more AI content.
```

**Issues:**
- No clear point or structure
- Random topic jumps
- "Also, I think..." breaks flow
- No connection between ideas
- Generic CTA unrelated to content

---

#### 0/3 Points: No Flow 💀
**Criteria:**
- Stream of consciousness
- No structure at all
- Contradicts itself
- Completely disorganized

---

### FLOW PATTERNS

#### Pattern 1: Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS)
```
Structure:
1. State problem
2. Agitate (make it hurt)
3. Present solution
4. Show proof
5. CTA

Example:
Problem: Lead scoring tools use generic rules.
Agitate: This means wasting time on bad-fit leads.
Solution: I built AI agent trained on MY ICP.
Proof: 91% accuracy vs 78% for generic tools.
CTA: Want the architecture? [Link]
```

**Best For:** Product solutions, tools, systems

---

#### Pattern 2: Before-After-Bridge (BAB)
```
Structure:
1. Before (pain state)
2. After (desired state)  
3. Bridge (how you got there)

Example:
Before: Manual qualification took 2 hours/day.
After: AI agent qualifies in 30 seconds.
Bridge: Built RAG system with Pinecone + Claude. [Details]
```

**Best For:** Personal transformation, case studies

---

#### Pattern 3: Story-Insight-Application (SIA)
```
Structure:
1. Story (what happened)
2. Insight (what you learned)
3. Application (how to apply)

Example:
Story: Day 47 - RAG system went down. Lost 6 hours.
Insight: Hybrid search would've caught the edge case.
Application: Here's how to implement hybrid search [Tutorial]
```

**Best For:** Lessons learned, building in public

---

### PACING GUIDE

**Too Fast (Rushed):**
```
❌ Built RAG. Used Pinecone. Works great. Try it.
```

**Too Slow (Dragging):**
```
❌ I've been thinking a lot about AI lately. There are many interesting developments in the field. One area that particularly fascinates me is...
```

**Just Right:**
```
✅ Built a RAG system. Cost: $40/month. Accuracy: 91%. Here's the architecture:
```

---

### TRANSITION QUALITY

**Smooth Transitions:**
```
✅ "The problem was chunking. Here's how I fixed it:"
✅ "Result: 91% accuracy. How it works:"
✅ "Why this matters:"
```

**Jarring Transitions:**
```
❌ "Also, another thing..."
❌ "By the way..."
❌ "Oh and I forgot to mention..."
```

## CATEGORY 3: CTA ANALYSIS (0-2 points)

### Definition
CTA (Call-to-Action) is what you want the reader to do next. Every piece of content should have ONE clear CTA.

---

### SCORING RUBRIC

#### 2/2 Points: Strong CTA ⭐⭐
**Criteria:**
- Clear, specific action
- Compelling reason WHY
- Connected to content
- Single, focused ask
- Easy to execute
- Audience-targeted

**Examples:**
```
✅ "Follow if you're building AI GTM systems. Tomorrow: Multi-LLM orchestration."
   - Action: Follow
   - Reason: You're building AI GTM + tomorrow's value
   - Easy: One click
   - Targeted: AI GTM builders

✅ "Built a free ICP calculator. Link in comments 👇"
   - Action: Click link
   - Reason: Free tool (valuable)
   - Easy: Link provided
   - Relevant: If you read about ICP, you need calculator

✅ "What's your biggest RAG challenge? Comment below—I'll analyze your setup."
   - Action: Comment
   - Reason: Personalized help offered
   - Easy: Type comment
   - Relevant: If you work with RAG
```

---

#### 1/2 Points: Weak CTA ⭐
**Criteria:**
- Vague action
- Weak or no reason
- Disconnected from content
- Too many asks

**Examples:**
```
⚠️ "Let me know your thoughts in the comments."
   - Generic request
   + At least asks for comments
   - No reason WHY to comment
   - What specific thoughts?

⚠️ "Follow for more AI content."
   + Clear action (follow)
   - Vague value ("more AI content" = every AI creator)
   - Not differentiated

⚠️ "Click the link in my bio to learn more."
   + Has action
   - No context (learn more about what?)
   - "In bio" adds friction
```

---

#### 0/2 Points: No/Bad CTA 💀
**Criteria:**
- No CTA at all
- Multiple competing CTAs
- Passive or apologetic
- Generic to point of useless

**Examples:**
```
🚫 "Thoughts?"
   - Most overused, laziest CTA on LinkedIn
   
🚫 "What do you think?"
   - Generic, no specific ask

🚫 [No CTA - post just ends]
   - Missed opportunity

🚫 "Follow me and click the link and comment and share and DM me and..."
   - Too many asks = reader does nothing
```

---

### CTA TYPES & WHEN TO USE

#### Type 1: Engagement CTAs (Best for LinkedIn)
**Goal:** Build audience, increase reach

```
✅ "Follow for daily AI systems content. Tomorrow: [specific topic]"
✅ "Repost if this resonated ♻️"
✅ "Comment 'RAG' and I'll DM you the full architecture"
✅ "Tag someone building AI GTM systems"
```

**When:** Building authority, growing audience

---

#### Type 2: Conversion CTAs (Lead Gen)
**Goal:** Capture leads, drive action

```
✅ "Built a free ICP calculator. Link in comments 👇"
✅ "Download the complete guide: [link]"
✅ "DM me 'AGENT' for the code repo"
✅ "Want this system? Book a 15-min walkthrough: [link]"
```

**When:** You have valuable asset to offer

---

#### Type 3: Discussion CTAs (Engagement)
**Goal:** Start conversation, learn from audience

```
✅ "What's your biggest RAG challenge? Drop it below."
✅ "Which would you choose: Pinecone or Weaviate? Why?"
✅ "Am I missing something? Tell me in comments."
```

**When:** You genuinely want audience input

---

#### Type 4: Preview CTAs (Build Anticipation)
**Goal:** Create return intent

```
✅ "Tomorrow: How I reduced API costs by 80%. Follow so you don't miss it."
✅ "Next week: Complete multi-agent architecture. Subscribe to newsletter."
✅ "Building in public: Day X/90. Follow the journey."
```

**When:** Serial content, building in public

---

### CTA MISTAKES TO AVOID

**1. Multiple CTAs**
```
❌ "Follow me for more. Also check out my website. And DM me if you want to chat. Oh and repost this."

✅ "Follow for daily AI systems builds. Tomorrow: LangGraph agents."
```

**2. Apologetic CTAs**
```
❌ "If you made it this far, maybe consider following?"
❌ "Sorry for the long post, but..."

✅ "Follow if you're building production AI systems."
```

**3. Selfish CTAs**
```
❌ "Follow me to grow my audience"
❌ "Like and share to help me out"

✅ "Follow for [specific value they'll get]"
```

**4. Vague CTAs**
```
❌ "Let's connect"
❌ "Reach out"
❌ "Learn more"

✅ "DM me 'SYSTEM' for the full tech stack breakdown"
```

## CATEGORY 4: TECHNICAL ACCURACY (0-2 points)

### Definition
Technical accuracy means claims are correct, terminology is proper, and examples are realistic.

---

### SCORING RUBRIC

#### 2/2 Points: Accurate ✅
**Criteria:**
- All technical claims are correct
- Terminology used properly
- Examples are realistic
- Numbers/stats are accurate
- No misleading statements

---

#### 1/2 Points: Minor Issues ⚠️
**Criteria:**
- Mostly accurate
- Some loose terminology
- Minor stat issues
- Examples slightly unrealistic

---

#### 0/2 Points: Inaccurate ❌
**Criteria:**
- Major technical errors
- Wrong terminology
- Impossible claims
- Misleading information

---

### COMMON AI/ML ERRORS

**Vector Databases:**
```
❌ "Pinecone is 10x faster than Weaviate"
   - Depends on use case, data, setup

✅ "In my benchmark (1M 1536-dim vectors, cosine similarity), Pinecone averaged 47ms vs Weaviate's 93ms"
   - Specific, qualified claim

❌ "Vector databases learn from your data"
   - They don't "learn" - they store/retrieve

✅ "Vector databases store embeddings and retrieve via similarity search"
```

**RAG Systems:**
```
❌ "RAG eliminates hallucinations"
   - RAG reduces but doesn't eliminate

✅ "RAG reduces hallucinations by grounding responses in retrieved context"

❌ "RAG is Retrieval-Augmented Generation"
   - Correct but doesn't explain

✅ "RAG: Retrieve relevant docs → inject as context → LLM generates grounded answer"
```

**LLMs:**
```
❌ "Claude is better than GPT-4"
   - Subjective, task-dependent

✅ "For long-context reasoning (100K+ tokens), Claude Opus outperformed GPT-4 in my tests"

❌ "AI learns in production"
   - Most deployments don't retrain

✅ "AI responses improve via prompt engineering and RAG, not real-time learning"
```

---

### GTM TERMINOLOGY

**Lead Qualification:**
```
✅ MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead) - Met marketing criteria
✅ SQL (Sales Qualified Lead) - Vetted by sales, ready for pipeline
❌ Confusing MQL with SQL

✅ "Our AI qualifies MQLs, then sales reviews for SQL conversion"
```

**Attribution:**
```
✅ First-touch: Credit to first interaction
✅ Last-touch: Credit to final interaction before conversion
✅ Multi-touch: Distributed credit across journey

❌ "We use AI attribution" - Too vague
✅ "We use time-decay attribution weighted toward bottom-of-funnel"
```

---

### VERIFICATION CHECKLIST

When reviewing technical content, check:

**Claims:**
- [ ] Are performance claims qualified with context?
- [ ] Are comparisons fair and specific?
- [ ] Are statistics cited or explainable?

**Terminology:**
- [ ] Is AI/ML terminology used correctly?
- [ ] Are acronyms defined first use?
- [ ] Are product names spelled correctly?

**Examples:**
- [ ] Are code examples syntactically correct?
- [ ] Are API calls realistic?
- [ ] Are architectures sound?

**Numbers:**
- [ ] Do costs match current pricing?
- [ ] Are metrics plausible?
- [ ] Are improvement claims reasonable?

## SCORING CALCULATION

### Formula
```
Total Score = Hook (0-3) + Flow (0-3) + CTA (0-2) + Technical (0-2)

Maximum: 10 points
```

### Score Interpretation

| Score | Grade | Quality | Action |
|-------|-------|---------|--------|
| 9-10 | A | Exceptional | Publish immediately |
| 7-8 | B | Strong | Minor tweaks (5-10 min) |
| 5-6 | C | Decent | Revision needed (15-30 min) |
| 3-4 | D | Weak | Major rework (1+ hour) |
| 0-2 | F | Poor | Start over |

---

## SPECIAL SCORING NOTES

### Bonus Consideration (Doesn't Add Points)
- Original research/data
- Unique perspective
- Exceptional formatting
- Multimedia (images, videos)

### Penalty Consideration (Can Reduce Score)
- Typos (professional content)
- Broken links
- Factual errors
- Spammy tone
