## Monday: GTM Engineering Execution

**Post time:** 8:00 AM IST  
**Target length:** 400-500 words  
**Tone:** Technical Authority  
**Audience:** GTM engineers, RevOps, operators  

### Template Structure

```
GTM Execution: [Specific System Built]

[HOOK: Client problem in 1-2 lines]

What we shipped:
→ [System/workflow 1]
→ [System/workflow 2]
→ [System/workflow 3]

[DEEP-DIVE: Pick one system and explain in detail]

[Optional: Screenshot or workflow diagram]

Results:
→ [Metric 1]: [Before] → [After]
→ [Metric 2]: [Before] → [After]
→ [Time/cost saved]

Stack:
[Tools used with specific roles]

Lesson learned:
[One key technical or strategic insight]

#GTMEngineering #RevOps #MarketingAutomation
```

### Hook Patterns

**Pattern 1: Problem quantification**
"Most Series A sales teams waste 15 hours/week manually qualifying leads."

**Pattern 2: Surprising stat**
"67% of pipeline in Series A SaaS companies is wrong-fit. Here's why:"

**Pattern 3: Contrarian insight**
"ICP scoring isn't a CRM problem. It's an enrichment problem."

### Body Guidelines

**What we shipped section:**
- Be specific (not "lead scoring" → "11-variable ICP scoring with multi-source validation")
- List 3 systems/workflows (builds credibility)
- Use action verbs (Built, Shipped, Integrated, Automated)

**Deep-dive section:**
- Pick the most interesting system
- Explain technical approach (3-5 sentences)
- Include tools used with specific roles
- Show architecture if complex

**Results section:**
- Always quantify (hours, %, $, conversion rate)
- Before → After format
- Include timeline context (Week 1, Month 1)
- Client context (Series A, 50 employees) without naming

**Stack section:**
- List 3-5 tools maximum
- Show how they connect
- Example: "Clay (enrichment) → n8n (orchestration) → HubSpot (CRM storage)"

### CTA Options

**Option 1: Newsletter**
"Full technical breakdown in this week's Production GTM newsletter → [link]"

**Option 2: Template**
"Template in comments (workflow JSON + setup guide)"

**Option 3: Engagement**
"What's your approach to [specific problem]?"

## Tuesday AM: Product Marketing Strategy

**Post time:** 8:00 AM IST  
**Target length:** 350-450 words  
**Tone:** Strategic Consultant  
**Audience:** Product marketers, founders, GTM strategists  

### Template Structure

```
Product Marketing: [Framework Name]

[HOOK: Common PM mistake or contrarian insight]

Here's the framework we use:

[FRAMEWORK NAME]

Step 1: [Action] → [Output]
Step 2: [Action] → [Output]
Step 3: [Action] → [Output]

Example in action:
[Real client scenario - sanitized]

Before framework:
→ [Problem metric]
→ [Pain point quantified]

After framework:
→ [Improved metric]
→ [Business impact]

Why this works:
[Strategic insight explaining the mechanism]

Template: [Download link or "DM 'framework' for template"]

#ProductMarketing #GTMStrategy #ICPStrategy #Positioning
```

### Hook Patterns

**Pattern 1: Contrarian wisdom**
"Most product marketers define ICP by company size. Wrong signal."

**Pattern 2: Hidden insight**
"The best positioning statements don't mention your product."

**Pattern 3: Common mistake**
"60% of B2B messaging talks about 'what' not 'why'. Here's the fix:"

### Framework Guidelines

**Naming:**
- Specific number (3-Layer ICP Framework, not "ICP Framework")
- Descriptive action (Signal Detection Framework, not "GTM Framework")
- Memorable (RICE, JTBD, not "Method A")

**Structure:**
- 3-5 steps maximum (more = overwhelming)
- Each step: Action → Output
- Linear progression (Step 1 enables Step 2)

**Example section:**
- Must be real (client or eKigai)
- Sanitize client details (Series A SaaS, not "Acme Corp")
- Include specific metrics
- Show clear before/after

### CTA Options

**Option 1: Lead magnet**
"Download the [Framework] template (Google Sheet): [link]"

**Option 2: Newsletter**
"Deep-dive methodology in this week's AI Product Marketer newsletter → [link]"

**Option 3: DM gate**
"DM 'framework' and I'll send you the template"

```

### Hook Patterns

**Pattern 1: Relatable pain**
"Most B2B SaaS founders miss their best prospects by days. Here's why:"

**Pattern 2: Surprising insight**
"We analyzed 500 funding announcements. 82% of founders reach out too late."

**Pattern 3: Challenge framing**
"Building real-time signal detection for eKigai. The challenge: speed vs accuracy."

### Critical Rules (70-20-10)

**70% Educational:**
- Lead with the learning/insight
- Explain the GTM engineering approach
- Share what worked and what didn't
- Technical depth (not marketing fluff)

**20% Product Context:**
- eKigai mentioned as the case study
- "Here's how we're solving it in eKigai"
- Feature as example, not promotion

**10% Soft CTA:**
- Invitation, not pressure
- "Testing with 50+ early users. Want early access?"
- NEVER: "Best tool", "Limited time", "Sign up now before..."

### Month-Specific CTAs

**Month 1:**
"Join 50+ founders on the eKigai waitlist → [link]

**Month 2:**
"eKigai early access is now open (limited to 100 founders).  
Book a 20-min demo → [calendar link]"

**Month 3:**
"eKigai is moving from beta to paid in 2 weeks.  
Lock in founder pricing ($299/month, normally $499) → [link]"

### What NOT to Say

❌ "eKigai is the best GTM tool on the market"
❌ "Revolutionary AI platform that will transform your business"
❌ "Limited time offer - only 10 spots left!"
❌ "Don't miss out on this opportunity"
❌ "Game-changing solution for modern founders"

✅ "Here's what we learned building eKigai's ICP scoring"
✅ "Testing this approach with 50 founders - early feedback is X"
✅ "Building in public: here's what worked and what didn't"

```

Built automated workflow:

Input: [Data source]
Process:
→ [Transformation step 1]
→ [Transformation step 2]
→ [Validation/routing step 3]
Output: [Final destination]

Stack:
→ [Tool 1]: [Specific role]
→ [Tool 2]: [Specific role]
→ [Tool 3]: [Specific role]

[Optional: Workflow screenshot or diagram]

Results Week 1:

### Hook Patterns

**Pattern 1: Time waste quantification**
"Client's sales team manually enriching 200 leads/week. Taking 12 hours total."

**Pattern 2: Process inefficiency**
"Most lead enrichment workflows fail at scale. Here's why:"

**Pattern 3: Cost comparison**

**Input section:**
- Specific trigger (HubSpot new lead, form fill, API call)

- Use arrows for visual flow
- Each step = one clear action
- Show decision points (if/then logic)

**Stack section:**
- Tool → Role format
- Show data flow between tools
- Mention specific features used

### Results Guidelines

**Always include:**
- Time metric (hours saved per week)
- Quality metric (accuracy, completeness)
- Scale metric (capacity increase)

**Context requirements:**
- Client size (Series A, 50 employees)
- Timeline (Week 1, Month 1)

```

### Hook Patterns

**Pattern 1: Contrarian observation**
"Most LinkedIn profiles are résumés. Nobody cares about your résumé."

"We tested two headline formats for a client. Same person. Different positioning."

### Results Section

**Format A vs Format B structure:**
```
Format A (Current approach):
→ Metric 1: [Value]
→ Metric 2: [Value]
→ Metric 3: [Value]

Format B (New approach):
→ Metric 1: [Value] (+X%)
→ Metric 2: [Value] (+Y%)

- "After 30 days:"
- "Week 1 results:"

**Must explain mechanism:**
- Not just "it worked"
- Why algorithm rewarded it
- Why audience responded
- What psychological trigger activated

"Why it worked: Value-driven headlines focus on customer outcome (not your credentials). Algorithm boosts posts from profiles with higher engagement history."

## Friday: Claude/Agentic Evaluation

**Post time:** 8:00 AM IST  
**Target length:** 350-450 words  
**Tone:** Technical Authority  
**Audience:** AI engineers, prompt engineers, technical builders  

### Template Structure

```
Agentic Evaluation: [Specific Quality Problem]

[HOOK: AI quality issue or evaluation challenge]

Built evaluation framework:

Test criteria:
→ [Metric 1]: [How it's measured]
→ [Metric 2]: [How it's measured]
→ [Metric 3]: [How it's measured]

Implementation:
[Code snippet or workflow description]

[Optional: LangChain/evaluation architecture]

Results:
→ Baseline quality: [Before metric]
→ After evaluation: [After metric]
→ Improvement: [% or absolute change]

Key lesson:
[Insight about AI evaluation or quality control]

#ClaudeAI #AgenticAI #AIEvaluation #PromptEngineering
```

### Hook Patterns

**Pattern 1: Quality problem**
"Outreach agent generating 'personalized' emails. Sales team said 40% felt generic."

**Pattern 2: Scale challenge**
"AI outputs look good in testing. Fail at scale. Here's why:"

**Pattern 3: Surprising finding**
"Most teams skip evaluation layers. That's where production quality lives."

### Test Criteria Section

**Format:**
```
Layer 1: [Quality dimension]
→ Test: [How measured]
→ Threshold: [Pass/fail criteria]
→ Gate: [What happens if fail]
```

**Example:**
```
Layer 1: Factual Accuracy
→ Test: Cross-check against source data
→ Threshold: 100% match required
→ Gate: Reject if any mismatch
```

### Implementation Section

**Include:**
- Framework/library used (LangChain, LangSmith, custom)
- Architecture overview (evaluation chain)
- Code snippet if helpful (10-15 lines max)
- Testing approach (sample size, methodology)

**Code snippet format:**
```python
# Brief context comment
eval_chain = (
    {"input": RunnablePassthrough()}
    | accuracy_check  # Layer 1
    | quality_scorer  # Layer 2
    | gate_function   # Pass/fail
)
```

### Key Lesson Section

**Focus areas:**
- What most teams miss about AI evaluation
- Why single-pass generation fails
- When to use LLM-as-judge vs rule-based
- Production vs development quality gaps

## Saturday: Product Development

**Post time:** 8:00 AM IST  
**Target length:** 300-400 words  
**Tone:** Strategic Consultant (Reflective)  
**Audience:** Product builders, founders, operators  

### Template Structure

```
Product Development: [Decision/Insight]

[HOOK: Product challenge or decision point]

What we're building:
[Feature or system description]

Why now:
→ [Customer signal or market need]
→ [Strategic importance]

Approach:
[Build plan or methodology]

Trade-offs considered:

Option A: [Description]
→ Pros: [List]
→ Cons: [List]
→ Cost: [Time/money]

Option B: [Description]
→ Pros: [List]
→ Cons: [List]
→ Cost: [Time/money]

Decision: [Chosen path]
Reasoning: [Why we chose this option]

Timeline: [When shipping]

#ProductDevelopment #BuildInPublic #ProductStrategy
```

### Hook Patterns

**Pattern 1: Build vs buy dilemma**
"Client needs real-time enrichment. Clay costs $800/month at their scale. Built custom instead."

**Pattern 2: Feature prioritization**
"Deciding what to build for eKigai Q2. Here's the framework:"

**Pattern 3: Technical decision**
"Choosing between serverless and container architecture for eKigai. Trade-offs:"

### Trade-offs Section

**Must include:**
- At least 2 options considered
- Honest pros/cons for each
- Cost implications (time, money, maintenance)
- Decision with clear reasoning

**Avoid:**
- Obvious choice (makes post useless)
- Hiding real trade-offs
- Ignoring long-term implications

### Why This Matters

**Saturday posts are different:**
- Lower promotional intent (pure product thinking)
- More reflective, less tactical
- Teaching decision-making, not promoting eKigai
- Can mention eKigai but not required
