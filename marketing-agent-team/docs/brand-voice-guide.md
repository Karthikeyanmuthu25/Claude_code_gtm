## ✅ On-Brand: The Way We Write

### Sentence Structure
- Short declarative sentences as the default
- Mix lengths: short punch + occasional longer explanation
- One idea per sentence. Then the next. Then build.
- Fragments are fine for emphasis. Like this.

### Paragraph Structure
- 1–3 sentences per paragraph maximum in LinkedIn/email
- White space is not wasted space — it's readability
- Blog: up to 4 sentences, then break

### Perspective
- First-person singular (`I`, `my`, `we`) — we write as a human, not a brand
- Address the reader directly: `you`, `your`, not "B2B marketers often..."
- POV is direct: "This is wrong." not "This might not always be optimal."

### Tone Markers We Use ✅
`[Fill in with your brand's actual phrases and patterns — examples below]`
- "Here's the thing:" — useful for setting up a counterpoint
- "The short answer:" — signals directness
- "Most [X] teams..." — grounds insights in the reader's reality
- "In our experience..." — positions as practitioner, not theorist
- "Worth saying out loud:" — signals a slightly uncomfortable truth

### Energy Level
- **LinkedIn**: Medium-high energy. Punchy. Creates forward momentum.
- **Email**: Calmer, more direct. One human talking to another.
- **Blog**: Thoughtful, structured. Authoritative but accessible.

## ❌ Off-Brand: What We Never Write

### Banned Phrases (absolute — never use these)
```
"I'm excited to share..."
"Thrilled to announce..."
"Game-changing"
"Synergy" / "Synergies"
"Leverage" (as a verb)
"Move the needle"
"Best-in-class"
"Cutting-edge"
"Seamless"
"Robust solution"
"In today's fast-paced world..."
"It goes without saying..."
"At the end of the day..."
```

### Banned Patterns
- Passive voice as the default ("It was decided..." → "We decided...")
- Hedging without reason ("could potentially possibly help...")
- Preamble before the point ("Before I get into the main topic, I wanted to first...")
- Corporate distance ("Users can leverage our platform to...")
- Faux modesty ("I'm no expert, but...")
- Cliffhanger abuse ("What happened next will shock you.")

### What We Avoid on LinkedIn Specifically
- Starting with "I" (LinkedIn algorithm issue + usually signals ego-led post)
- Posting without a specific, concrete insight
- Ending every post with a generic "What do you think?" CTA
- Gratuitous tags of people who aren't genuinely relevant
- More than 3 hashtags

## 📝 Writing Examples

### ✅ On-Brand LinkedIn Opening Lines
```
"Most content calendars are a lie."
"We tested 47 LinkedIn hooks last quarter. Here's what we learned."
"Nobody tells you that the brief is where most content fails."
"Your ICP isn't reading your blog. Here's what they're reading instead."
"The thing about AI-generated content isn't that it sounds like AI."
```

### ❌ Off-Brand LinkedIn Opening Lines
```
"Excited to share some insights about content marketing!"
"In today's digital landscape, B2B companies face unique challenges..."
"As a thought leader in the space, I've been reflecting on..."
"Happy [day of week] everyone! Quick thought on content strategy..."
```

### ✅ On-Brand Email Opening Lines
```
"Quick question before I get to the point:"
"We built something that fixed a problem I kept complaining about."
"[Company name] showed up in our research — here's what we found."
"This is a cold email. I'll make it worth 30 seconds."
```

### ❌ Off-Brand Email Opening Lines
```
"I hope this email finds you well."
"My name is [name] and I'm reaching out because..."
"I wanted to touch base regarding..."
"As a fellow professional in the [industry] space..."
```

## 🔤 Vocabulary Guide

### Words We Use ✅
`[Fill in with your brand's actual preferred vocabulary]`
- **Content**: "post", "piece", "article" — not "content asset"
- **Customers**: "customers", "clients", "teams" — not "users" or "end users"
- **Product**: `[your product name]` — always, not "the platform" or "our solution"
- **Process**: "workflow", "system", "approach" — not "methodology" or "framework" (unless explaining a specific one)
- **Results**: "outcomes", "results", "what happened" — not "ROI optimization" or "value delivery"

### Words We Avoid ❌
- "Utilize" → use "use"
- "Facilitate" → use "help" or "enable"
- "Regarding" → use "about"
- "Prior to" → use "before"
- "In order to" → use "to"
- "Leverage" (verb) → use "use", "apply", "build on"

## 🧩 Channel-Specific Voice Notes

### LinkedIn
- More direct and punchy than any other channel
- It's okay to be a little provocative — LinkedIn rewards takes
- The personal "I" experience is valued — we write from experience
- Dry wit is welcome, but never at the audience's expense

### Email (Cold)
- Even more stripped back than LinkedIn
- No performance — just one person talking to another
- The goal is a reply, not applause
- Less is always more

### Email (Nurture)
- Slightly warmer than cold — these people chose to hear from us
- Educational and generous — give before you ask
- Still concise — nobody asked for an essay

### Blog
- The most polished channel, but still us
- We can use subheadings, data, examples — but the voice stays the same
- We don't suddenly become "a leading provider of..."
- Every post should feel like it was written by a person who knows this deeply

## 🚨 Brand Voice Red Lines (Escalate to Human)

These situations require a human review — do not publish:
1. Any content that makes a specific quantitative claim we can't verify (e.g., "increases revenue by 40%")
2. Any content that positions directly against a named competitor
3. Any content that references a customer, client, or case study not in the approved library
4. Any content that takes a political position, even implicitly
5. Any content that the brand-voice-checker rates as OFF-BRAND after two revision cycles
