## Email 1 — The Reframe

**Subject line options** (A/B test these):
- A: Your first 10 customers vs. your ICP
- B: Why your outbound isn't converting (it's not the copy)
- C: The GTM mistake hiding inside your ICP doc

**Preview text**: Most founders have an ICP. Almost none have an Early Customer Persona.

---

**Body**:

Hi [First name],

Quick question — and it's uncomfortable for most founders I talk to:

Do you know who actually bought your first 10–20 deals, and what specifically made them say yes?

Not your ICP. Your *actual early buyers*. Their real job context, the specific pain that was acute enough to act on, the moment they decided to take the meeting.

Most founders have an ICP document. Firmographics, use cases, maybe some JTBD language. It looks right. It came from the first deals.

But an ICP and an Early Customer Persona are not the same thing — and conflating them is one of the quietest GTM killers at the pre-Series A stage.

The ICP is where you're going.
The Early Customer Persona (ECP) is who's actually buying right now — and what your GTM needs to be built on.

When messaging isn't landing and outbound isn't converting, the problem is almost never the copy.
It's that the GTM is optimized for the future customer, not the real one.

Worth thinking about.

## Email 2 — The Framework

**Subject line options**:
- A: How to build an Early Customer Persona (3-step method)
- B: The $1M ARR persona vs. the $10M ARR persona
- C: Why your first sales hire can't close the same deals you closed

**Preview text**: Your ECP and ICP serve different moments. Here's how to build one from scratch.

---

**Body**:

Hi [First name],

Last email I shared the distinction between an Early Customer Persona and an ICP.

Today I want to make it practical — because the ECP isn't a strategy document. It's a conversion tool.

Here's how to build one from the customers you already have:

**Step 1 — Identify your real early buyers**
Pull your first 10–30 closed deals. Not the ones you wanted. The ones that actually happened. Who are these people, really? Title, company stage, funding, team size at time of purchase.

**Step 2 — Map the triggering moment**
For each buyer: what was happening at their company in the 30–90 days before they bought? A new hire, a board push, a failed campaign, a competitor win? The trigger is more diagnostic than the firmographic.

**Step 3 — Extract the exact language**
What words did they use on the first call to describe their problem? Not your words — theirs. "Not converting," "wrong fit," "we keep changing the messaging" — these phrases are more valuable than any positioning exercise.

The output is not a slide deck.
It's a one-page document that answers: who buys, why now, and what language makes them recognize their pain.

This is what your outbound copy, your SDR call script, and your channel decisions should be built on — not the ICP.

The reason your first sales hire struggles to close the same deals you did? They're working from the ICP. You were (unconsciously) working from your ECP.

## Notes for QA / Brand Check

- Email 1 is deliberately short and single-insight. Do not add more content.
- Email 2 is the highest-value email — the 3-step framework should be scannable, not dense prose.
- Email 3 is conversational, not promotional. The CTA is a reply or a follow — not a demo booking.
- Exact language from audience insights used intentionally: "not converting," "wrong fit," "messaging isn't landing," "first 10 customers," "GTM motion," "first sales hire can't close."
- Subject line variants are included for A/B testing — final selection deferred to ab-test-designer agent.
