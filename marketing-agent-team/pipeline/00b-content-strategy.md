# Content Strategy Plan
**Campaign**: Early Customer Persona vs ICP — B2B GTM Clarity Campaign
**Business Goal**: Thought leadership / brand awareness — grow audience of B2B SaaS founders and PMMs by delivering a reframing insight that builds credibility and drives follows
**Date**: 2026-03-17
**Strategist**: content-strategist agent

---

## 🎯 Strategic Recommendation Summary

This campaign owns a specific, underserved insight in the GTM content space: the Early Customer Persona and the ICP are not the same thing, and conflating them is a silent GTM killer. We lead with the mistake (founders build GTM for a customer who doesn't exist yet), teach the distinction through a concrete framework, and position the brand as the practitioner voice on early-stage GTM clarity. Full channel coverage (LinkedIn × 3, email × 3, blog × 1) is justified here — each format serves a distinct role in reaching and deepening with the same ICP.

---

## 📌 Content Pillar Focus
- **Primary pillar**: B2B content strategy & GTM — this campaign is core GTM methodology, directly relevant to the primary ICP pain point
- **Secondary pillar**: Founder brand building on LinkedIn — the LinkedIn variants double as thought leadership equity for a founder voice
- ⚠️ **Out of scope for this campaign**: AI-powered marketing workflows, marketing automation & efficiency — do not let the AI-acceleration angle (mentioned in background context) become the headline; it's supporting context only

---

## 🔽 Funnel Stage
- **Stage**: TOFU (Top of Funnel)
- **Reader's awareness level**: Problem-unaware to Problem-aware — most founders believe they *have* an ICP; they don't know the ECP distinction exists or matters
- **Content goal**: Shift the reader's mental model. After consuming this content, they should think: "I've been calling my early buyers my ICP — and that's been causing my GTM drift. I need to separate these." The CTA is a follow, not a demo.

---

## 📐 Recommended Content Set

| Format | Agent | Priority | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn post (3 variants) | linkedin-writer | Must-have | Primary distribution channel for B2B SaaS founders; framework-style posts with "founders get this wrong" hooks have proven traction per brief history; 3 variants lets us test hook formats |
| Blog post (SEO-optimized) | blog-writer | Must-have | "Early customer persona B2B GTM" is an ownable keyword cluster with low competition; most content conflates ECP/ICP — this post can rank and compound over time |
| Cold email sequence (3-part) | email-writer | High | Enables direct reach to founders and PMMs beyond organic; the sequence should educate-first, not pitch-first, anchored to the same ECP vs ICP insight |

**Minimum viable set**: LinkedIn posts (3 variants) + blog post. These two assets cover the broadest reach and leave a lasting SEO asset. Email is high-value but can be sequenced after if bandwidth is constrained.

---

## 💡 Recommended Content Angle

- **Angle**: "The $1M ARR persona is not the $10M ARR persona — and using the wrong one kills your GTM before it starts"
- **Tension**: Founders believe they need to nail their ICP to build GTM. The real insight: you need to nail your *Early Customer Persona first* — a different, more specific, and more urgent artifact than the ICP
- **Proof anchor**: The observable pattern that early-stage founders build outreach, messaging, and channels for a "future ideal customer" while ignoring the actual signals from their first 10–50 buyers — causing messaging drift, wrong channel bets, and stalled pipeline
- **Why we can own this**: The ECP vs ICP distinction is a practitioner-level GTM insight. Most ICP content is template-driven and generic. This angle is specific, counterintuitive, and directly actionable — teachable in a LinkedIn post and defensible in a 2,000-word blog post

## 📣 Distribution Plan

### LinkedIn Post (3 Variants)
- **Primary**: Publish on LinkedIn — founder/PMM personal brand account; target organic reach in B2B SaaS GTM circles
- **Secondary**: Reshare the top-performing variant in a relevant Slack community or newsletter mention within 48 hours of publish
- **Timing**: Publish Tuesday or Wednesday, 8–9am local time (peak B2B LinkedIn engagement window); stagger variants across 1–2 weeks to A/B test naturally
- **Engagement play**: Author replies to every comment within the first 2 hours of posting to boost algorithmic reach; pin best-performing variant to profile

### Blog Post
- **Primary**: Publish on brand blog with full SEO optimization; target keywords: "early customer persona B2B GTM", "ICP vs early customer persona", "ideal customer profile framework"
- **Secondary**: Repurpose key sections into LinkedIn carousel or second LinkedIn post; excerpt the framework as a standalone LinkedIn native doc
- **Timing**: Publish same week as LinkedIn posts (by 2026-03-22 to allow indexing before deadline); cross-link from LinkedIn post in comments, not caption
- **Engagement play**: Submit to relevant newsletters (e.g., GTM-focused founder roundups); add internal CTA to follow for more frameworks

### Cold Email Sequence (3-Part)
- **Primary**: Target B2B SaaS Founders and PMMs via direct outreach; sequence should be educate-first — Email 1: insight/reframe, Email 2: framework detail + example, Email 3: soft CTA (follow / reply with question)
- **Secondary**: Repurpose Email 1 as a LinkedIn DM script for warm connections
- **Timing**: Launch sequence same week as LinkedIn publish; send Email 1 on publish day to align with content momentum
- **Engagement play**: Personalize Email 1 subject line with a founder-specific signal (company stage, recent funding, job title); reply personally to any response within 24 hours

## 📚 Learning Integration

No historical campaign data yet — this is the first run. Recommend tracking the following metrics to build the learnings log:

- **LinkedIn**: Which hook format drives highest engagement rate (numbered framework vs. "founders get this wrong" opener vs. story-led)?
- **Email**: Which subject line pattern achieves >35% open rate? Which email in the 3-part sequence gets the most replies?
- **Blog**: Time-on-page and scroll depth as proxies for content quality; keyword ranking position at 30 and 60 days
- **Cross-channel**: Do readers who engage with LinkedIn also convert to blog sessions? (use UTM tracking on blog link shared in LinkedIn comments)
