## Variant A — Mistake Hook

Most founders build their GTM for a customer who doesn't exist yet.

They write an ICP. They build outbound sequences. They pick channels.
And then wonder why messaging isn't landing and pipeline is soft.

The problem isn't execution.
They skipped a step: defining the Early Customer Persona (ECP) before the ICP.

These are not the same thing:

→ ECP: Who actually buys first. Real buyers, real context, real constraints.
→ ICP: Who you want to sell to at scale. Aspirational. Built for growth.

Building your GTM from an ICP when you have 5–15 customers means you're messaging to a persona built on assumptions — not signal.

Your first 10 customers are not a stepping stone to your ICP.
They're the signal.

The ECP gives you what no ICP template can:
the exact language, triggers, and objections of the people who already bought.

Get that right before you build the system.

If your outbound isn't converting, here's one diagnostic question:
Is your GTM built around your early buyers — or around the customer you're hoping to find? #GTM #B2BSaaS #GoToMarket

## Variant B — Contrarian Reframe

Your ICP isn't the problem.

Building a full GTM motion before you know your Early Customer Persona is.

Most founders conflate the two. It's one of the most expensive mistakes in early-stage GTM — not because the ICP is wrong, but because it's the wrong artifact for the wrong stage.

Here's the distinction that changes everything:

1. ECP answers: Who buys right now, under what conditions, and why?
2. ICP answers: Who do we optimize for at scale, and what does that customer look like?

One is built from real conversion signal.
The other is built from where you want to go.

When outbound isn't converting and you don't know if it's the copy or the targeting — it's almost always the ECP that's missing.

Your ICP is a destination.
Your ECP is the map for getting there.

Most early-stage B2B SaaS companies have one and not the other.

PMMs: this is also why the ICP doc you inherited from your founder feels right but doesn't produce sharp messaging. It was never grounded in ECP data.

What did your first 10–20 customers have in common that your current ICP doesn't capture? #B2BSaaS #StartupGTM #FounderLed

## Variant C — Stakes / ARR Hook

The persona that gets you to $1M ARR will stall you at $3M.

Not because it's wrong.
Because it was built for a different stage — and most founders never update it.

Here's what happens at almost every early-stage B2B SaaS company:

→ Founder closes first deals via network and hustle
→ Builds an ICP from those deals
→ Hires first SDR, builds outbound, scales the motion
→ Results drop. SDR can't close the same deals. Wrong-fit leads pile up.

The root cause is almost never the sales process.
It's that the first GTM motion was built on an Early Customer Persona — real signal from real buyers — and the scaled motion got built on an ICP that never matched it.

ECP and ICP serve different strategic moments:
→ ECP: Who buys first, and what makes them say yes today
→ ICP: Who you build the company around for the next 3–5 years

Skipping ECP doesn't save time.
It means your outbound tools, SDR salaries, and ad spend are all pointed at the wrong person.

The $1M to $10M ARR jump isn't about better GTM execution.
It's about getting the right persona for each stage of that journey.

At what point did your GTM motion stop working — and did you ever trace it back to the persona? #GTM #SaaS #B2BSaaS #GoToMarket
