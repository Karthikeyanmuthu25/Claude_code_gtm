## 04-linkedin-draft.md — LinkedIn Posts

### Overall: PASS

The LinkedIn posts are the strongest brand voice execution of the three assets. The tone is direct, peer-level, and practitioner-forward. All three variants lead with a recognizable mistake or tension — not a definition or concept. The use of arrow (→) to separate framework items is appropriate for the format and avoids bullet-list formality.

**Variant A — Mistake Hook**: PASS

- No passive voice, no corporate speak, no jargon

**Variant B — Contrarian Reframe**: PASS

**Variant C — Stakes / ARR Hook**: PASS WITH MINOR DEVIATION
- Minor deviation: "It means your outbound tools, SDR salaries, and ad spend are all working from the wrong signal." — the phrase "working from the wrong signal" is slightly abstract. The brand voice calls for concrete, grounded language.
- Recommended fix: Revise to: "It means your outbound tools, SDR salaries, and ad spend are pointed at the wrong person." — more concrete and visceral.
- Otherwise: The ARR narrative is strong, the before/after scenario is well-grounded, and the closing question is specific.

## 05-email-draft.md — Cold Outreach Sequence

### Overall: PASS

The email sequence maintains the educate-first, practitioner-to-peer tone throughout. Email 1 is deliberately short and earns trust by not pitching. Email 2 delivers a practical framework without feeling like a whitepaper. Email 3 is genuinely conversational and ends with an open question rather than a disguised pitch.

**Email 1 — The Reframe**: PASS
- "Not your ICP. Your actual early buyers." — italics and directness are on-brand
- Single-insight structure is correct for Email 1
- No jargon, no passive voice, no corporate speak

**Email 2 — The Framework**: PASS WITH MINOR DEVIATION
- The phrase "The output is not a slide deck or a persona template. It is a one-page working document..." — good. But the preceding sentence "The output is a one-page document that answers:" repeats the same structure and weakens the rhythm.
- Recommended fix: Cut the repeated sentence. Keep: "The output is not a slide deck or a persona template. It's a one-page working document that answers..." — tighter and more punchy.
- Minor: "Step 3 — Extract the exact language" uses "verbatim" ("Write them down verbatim") — this is mildly formal. Consider: "Write them down word-for-word."
- Overall framework is scannable and practitioner-grounded — good brand execution

**Email 3 — The Soft CTA**: PASS
- "Not a pitch. Just want to know where you're at." — this is strong, brand-aligned, and human
- The closing CTA ("Reply to this email if you'd rather just talk through where you're stuck") is appropriately low-pressure
- No brand voice issues

## 06-blog-draft.md — Long-Form Blog Post

### Overall: PASS WITH NOTE

The blog post is the most challenging format to keep on-brand because long-form content tends to drift toward formal/academic language. This draft largely avoids that trap. The consistent use of short declarative sentences, concrete examples, and real-world GTM scenarios keeps the voice practitioner-level throughout.

**Strengths:**
- The opening question ("Am I building this company for a customer I wish existed — or for the one who actually buys?") is a perfect brand-aligned opener — direct, honest, and resonant with the target audience
- The "triggering context gets stripped out" section reads like a practitioner explaining something they've seen repeatedly — exactly the right tone
- "The founder was unconsciously selling from their ECP. The SDR is working from the ICP. They will always produce different results." — this is the best line in the post and the strongest brand voice moment

**Minor deviation:**
- The phrase "This is an inherently aspirational artifact" (referring to the ICP) uses slightly formal language. The brand voice calls for plain, direct construction.
- Recommended fix: Revise to: "The ICP is inherently aspirational." — drop "artifact" and keep the directness.

**Style recommendation (not a deviation — optional improvement):**
- The "What the ECP Changes Downstream" section lists 5 areas (copy, channels, SDR onboarding, product roadmap, investor conversations). The investor conversations entry feels like it extends the post's scope slightly beyond the primary audience concern — founders at this stage are more focused on selling than on investor signaling.
- Recommendation: Consider moving "Investor conversations" to a parenthetical note rather than a full bullet point, or dropping it entirely to tighten the section's focus. Not a brand voice issue — a relevance recommendation.
