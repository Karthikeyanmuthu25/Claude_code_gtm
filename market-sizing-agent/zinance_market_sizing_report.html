Understanding the Numbers

A plain-language breakdown of TAM, SAM, and SOM — and what they mean for Zinance

TAM Total Addressable Market  ·  $17.3B
"The total size of the entire market Zinance could serve if it reached every possible customer in the US."
  • Covers the entire US Finance & Accounting Outsourcing industry — bookkeeping, tax compliance, CFO services, and financial ops
  • ~6.5 million US businesses fall into this category (SBA 2024 data)
  • The market is currently worth $17.3 billion and growing at 8.9% per year
  • Projected to reach $29B+ by 2030 — driven by AI, talent scarcity, and variable-cost preferences
SAM Serviceable Addressable Market  ·  $3.1B
"The slice of the total market that Zinance can realistically target, given who they actually serve."
  • Filtered to US companies in tech, SaaS, and e-commerce only — with 10 to 200 employees
  • Group 1: 18,000–25,000 VC-backed startups actively funded at seed–Series B
  • Group 2: 90,000–100,000 high-growth bootstrapped SaaS & e-commerce companies
  • Removed ~40–45% unlikely to switch to an outsourced model → leaves ~65,000 reachable companies
  • At $4,000/month average contract value ($48K/year), this equals a $3.1B opportunity
SOM Serviceable Obtainable Market  ·  $62M → $144M ARR
"The realistic revenue Zinance can actually win in the next 3–5 years."
  • Benchmarked against Pilot.com — best-funded competitor ($222M raised, ~1,000 clients, ~$27M ARR after 7 years)
  • Zinance needs just 2% of SAM by Year 3 and 4.6% by Year 5 — conservative targets
  • Year 3: 1,300 clients × $48K/year = $62M ARR
  • Year 5: 3,000 clients × $48K/year = $144M ARR
  • Bench's 2024 exit freed up 10,000+ displaced customers — immediate market opportunity
